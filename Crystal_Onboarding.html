CRYSTAL USER FLOW & ONBOARDING

BACKGROUND & ROLE

Crystal is a new email technology based on empathy. Through the use of public information, the program aims to help users communicate more effectively, and efficiently, by providing email, speaking and selling tips tailored to recipients preferred communication styles and personalities.

The apprenticeship team at Fresh Tilled Soil worked with the founder in the weeks before Crystalâ€™s public launch to audit its user onboarding process in hopes increasing signups for trials, and converting trial participants into full time paying users.

I contributed to all of the research, wireframe creation and execution of the client presentation. I was also the client liason for the group and project manager focused on maintaining the timeline and producing deliverables on time.

REVISED USER FLOW

The process began with a usability audit that mapped the current onboarding flow for ease of use and clarity, mental model interviews to attain insights on communication styles, personality and privacy concerns and interviews with current early stage users.

From these insights, and research on onboarding from publications such as Hooked by Nir Eyal and Designing for Emotion by Trevor van Gorp and Edie Adams, hypotheses were then drawn around how to transform the process and increase consumers likelihood to onboard and use the product.

WIREFRAMING & PROTOTYPING

The research findings were then converted into low fidelity wireframes, prototyped in Invision, and presented to the client for implementation.
