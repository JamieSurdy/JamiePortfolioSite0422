NONPROFIT UX/UI REDESIGN

BACKGROUND & ROLE

Tutoring Plus is a Cambridge based non-profit that serves under privileged children through education and enrichment programs. I, along with two web developers and two photographers, were tasked with redesigning their site in order to increase student enrollment, volunteer participation and increase donor funding

This was a dual track project with both usability need as well as a visual redesign.

For this redesign, I served as the UX/UI Designer, as well as the project manager between the internal team (developers and photographers) and the main client contact.

USER EXPERIENCE PROCESS

Usability goals for this project included creating clear call to actions that would be consistent across the site, analyzing content and revising the site map, simplifying the lengthy volunteer and participant form, and providing an easy pathway to donation on each page.

The UX process included user research resutling in the creation of user personas and scenarios, a revised sitemap, low fidelity wireframes, and annotated wireframes for developers to work off of.

USER INTERFACE REDESIGN

Visual goals were centered on creating a cleaner look and feel, while maintaining a fun, yet professional, tone.

In order to make the site more visually appealing, while maintaining brand recognition, the original blue, green and orange color palette was modernized. Instead of neon hues of each of these colors, which felt outdated and aggressive, bright colors in the same scheme were chosen to maintain the upbeat tone of the group.
