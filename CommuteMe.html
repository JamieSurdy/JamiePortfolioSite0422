COMMUTE ME UX DESIGN SPRINT

BACKGROUND & ROLE

As part of the application process for the Fresh Tilled Soil User Experience Apprenticeship program, a select group of finalists were asked to attend a daylong bootcamp focused on UX design sprints. During the course of the day, we were told that we would have one week to complete a design sprint that would result in a product aimed at solving a problem related to travel.

Before breaking off to work independently for the week, we partook in a whiteboarding brainstorming session around problems individuals and companies face when it comes to travel. We then broke off and were given the week to focus ona specific problem and create a product solution to solve it.

FIVE DAY DESIGN SPRINT

I chose to focus on commuting, specifically how it relates to, and can be eased via, the sharing economy. I structured my week around Google Ventureâ€™s five day design sprints, each day given a specific task (Understand, Diverge, Decide, Prototype, Validate).

My competitive analysis, user tests, personas, empathy maps, problem/solution sets, and sketches led me to create a mobile application to marry drivers willing to give rides with passengers willing to pay for transportation to and from work.

WIREFRAME & PROTOTYPE

Once the research phase was completed, I created low fidelity wireframes which ultimately were input into an Invision prototype for further user testing of my hypotheses.

Key findings concluded that safety and ease of use would be the most important factors for application adoption for users, and next steps would surely have been testing on a larger sample.
