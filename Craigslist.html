UX CASE STUDY

BACKGROUND & ROLE

Everyone knows Craigslist. What it is. What it does.

Everyone also knows it is not fun to use, and it’s ugly. But that’s Craigslist, right? Well, this one was a passion project of mine.

How, with very little design (to maintain the site’s authenticity), could the user experience be enhanced?

Looking for a challenge, I decided to tackle the site that is Craigslist and create a UX Case Study around the experience.

I focused on improving usability by making simple changes that have a big impact, while not forgetting the importance of brand recognition (ugly, but loved) and users’ mental models around navigating and using the site.

Then, just for fun, I made it look prettier (come on Craigslist!).

USER EXPERIENCE PROCESS

After attempting to identify users (via personas) and user flows, I quickly realized the number of users and things they could do was seemingly endless, so I tailored my thinking to focus on what the simple changes were that impact every user. I found them to be…

  • Account Management- Messy and Confusing
  • Navigation- inconsistent across pages or categories, and absent completely in some areas
  • Search- not available on all pages, advanced filters only available in certain and pages

ACCOUNT & SEARCH SECTIONS

From that discovery, I set about creating a new information architecture which includes a dual navigation scheme that would be fixed on all pages of the site, creating clear paths to information (no more dreaded back button for every action!).

I then tacked the Account section of the site. I made it accessible from all pages so a user could clearly find things they’ve looked at, saved, messages that have been sent to them, etc. In essence, I made it easy to track Craigslist activity.

Finally, I created a consistent search bar on the top of the page with advanced options available by category on the left (expandable and collapsible for ease of use).
