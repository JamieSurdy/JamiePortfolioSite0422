RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis- Assess the strengths and weaknesses of competitors against user's needs. Understand what exists in the market and where new products can fill holes and provide benefits.

Mental Models- Capture user behavior, pain points, motivations, and needs from contextual inquires.

Map out product features to identify gaps, opportunities, and assumptions to test based on models.

Identify user's thought process and desired reactions toward each task flow segment. Creates empathy upfront and a reminder of the thoughts of the user as design decisions are made.

STORYBOARDS & WIREFRAMING

Storyboards- Visually depict scenarios with storyboards. The storyboard should show the context, the problem, the user's fears/motivations, and how the problem will be solved. I use tools like Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign and Sketch to quickly create these user flows.

Wireframes- Visually communicate abstract concepts, interactions and content heirarchies with users and team members to generate feedback. Wireframes allow the team to share IA plans with clients and discuss how to map interactions before delving into design decisions.

PROTOTYPING

Range of prototypes based on budget and timelines (paper prototyping, Photoshop, Sketch, Invision).

Uncover usability issues and validate design decisions based on actual user reactions.

Higher fidelity prototypes allow easy distribution and the ability to observe exactly how well the product will be received before being built. Clients also get to understand simple interactions early on.

DESIGN & DEVELOPMENT

Apply product branding, grid structure, typography, color scheme, and icons.

Create clean visual designs that allow the underlaying experience to shine through and create a desired tone and connection with the user.

Visual designs are created using Photoshop, Illustrator, and Sketch3.
